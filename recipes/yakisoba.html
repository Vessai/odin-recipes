Yakisoba

Description

This is classic Japanese meal more or less translates to "Fried noodles", and is exactly what it says on the tin. An easy meal that you can chuck whatever's left in your fridge into and it'll probably be a success.

Ingredients

  • Noodles (ideally soba, udon are also tasty!), 1/2 nest per portion
  • Carrot, 1 per portion
  • Spring onion, 1 per portion
  • Any other vegetables you think would work
  • Sesame oil, 1/2 tablespoon per portion (can be replaced with a neutral oil and additional sesame seeds)
  • Soy sauce, 3/4 tablespoon per portion
  • Mirin, 1/2 tablespoon per portion
  • Cooking sake, 1/4 tablespoon per portion
  • Sesame seeds, 1/2 tablespoon per portion
  • A few pieces of sushi ginger

Recipe

  1. Prepare the vegetables, leaving the green parts of the spring onions separate for later.
  2. Fry the vegetables in the oil at a high heat until well browned.
  3. While the vegetables are frying, cook the noodles as described on the packet.
  4. Keeping the frying pan on a medium-high heat, add the soy sauce, mirin and cooking sake, allowing the sauce to caramelise a bit.
  5. After draining the cooked noodles, add them to the frying pan and mix together for 3 minutes or so, until well coated and fried.
  6. Turn off the heat and add sesame seeds, serving with the remaining parts of the spring onions and sushi ginger as a garnish.
  7. Enjoy!
